Phishing Prevention & Awareness

Educate yourself to stay ahead of cyber threats.

Check the URL

Always verify the domain name. Phishers often use slight misspellings (e.g., "g0ogle.com" instead of "google.com"). Look for the lock icon and HTTPS.

Suspicious Emails

Be wary of urgent requests, generic greetings ("Dear Customer"), and unexpected attachments. Official organizations rarely ask for passwords via email.

Enable 2FA

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of security. Even if a phisher gets your password, they won't have the second code.

Use Safe DNS

Configure your network to use filtered DNS services like Cloudflare (1.1.1.2) or OpenDNS/Cisco Umbrella to block known malicious sites at the network level.

Quick Tip

"If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is." - Phishing often lures victims with promises of money, prizes, or urgent account warnings.
